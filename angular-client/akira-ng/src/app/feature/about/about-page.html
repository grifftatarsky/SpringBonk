About

What it is

Akira is a small web app for running ranked-choice votes on books. You make shelves, put books on them, start an election, nominate a few candidates, and everyone orders their picks. An instant-runoff count tells you what wins. No spreadsheets. No "wait, who voted?" No group-chat doom loop at 11pm on a Wednesday.

Why it exists

I run a book club called Bonk! and we couldn't agree on what to read. Picking a book by "loudest person wins" is annoying. Picking by simple majority throws away everybody's second choices. Ranked-choice keeps everybody's opinions in the count, and the answer feels earned. So I built the thing.

Bonk! is the book club. Akira is what the book club runs on. You can use Akira for your own book club, or any other thing you'd vote on in a group where the answer matters more than the process. (I'm not going to pretend it generalizes to neighborhood elections. It's a book-club app.)

What's here right now

  • Shelves. Named lists of books. You can have as many as you want. Books come from Open Library or you type them in.
  • Elections. Make one, give it a title, optionally an end date. Status is Open, Closed, or Indefinite.
  • Nominations. Pull books from a shelf, search Open Library, or type one in. Any member can nominate.
  • Ranked ballots. Drag or arrow-button your way to a preference order. Auto-saved as you go.
  • Results. When the election closes, you see the winner and the round-by-round count that got you there.

What's not here (yet)

  • Notifications (coming).
  • Groups / multi-club scoping. Right now it's one-club-per-instance by convention.
  • Non-plurality tabulation methods. Only instant-runoff today.
  • Generalizing past books. The models say "Book" everywhere for now.

How it's built

Angular on the front, Spring Boot on the back, Keycloak for auth, NGINX in between, Postgres underneath, Docker wrapping it all. Deploy on a laptop, a VPS, or a cluster — nothing fancy. Source is on GitHub.
